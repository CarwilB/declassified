Subject: Bolivian Delegation To Third Un Conference On Law Of The Sea
TAGS: PBOR, UN, BL, PINR
Ref: STATE 67788; LA PAZ 1791; LA PAZ 1617
  1. BOLIVIAN DELEGATION TO THIRD UN LOS CONFERENCE WAS APPROVED BY PRESIDENT BANZER ON MAY 28. CHIEF OF DELEGATION WILL BE GUSTAVO MEDEIROS QUERJAZU, EX-FOREIGN MINISTER AND CURRENTLY A MEMBER OF GOB’S MARITIME COMMISSION. IN LIKELY EVENT THAT MEDEIROS DOES NOT REMAIN IN CARACAS FOR ENTIRE CONFERENCE, ALTER- NATE CHIEF OF DELEGATION: DR. FELIPE TREDINNICK ABASTO, WILL HEAD BOLIVIAN TEAM. TREDINNICK ASSUMED POST AS DIRECTOR OF LOS DEPARTMENT OF FOREIGN MINISTRY IN APRIL AND IS BOLIVIA’S LEADING EXPERT ON TECHNICAL QUESTIONS INVOLVING LAW OF THE SEA. OTHER MEMBERS OF BOLIVIA’S FIVE-MAN DELEGATION ARE SERGIO PALACIOS, MANUEL FRONTAURA ARGANDONA AND VICTRO TORRES ACHA. ALL MEMBERS OF DELEGATION HOLD PERSONAL RANK OF AMBASSADOR EXCEPT TORRES, WHO AS SECRETARY-GENERAL OF DELEGATION

WILL HOLD TITLE OF MINISTER-COUNDELOR.

  1. FOLLOWING BIOGRAPHIC DATA ARE PROVIDED ON MEMBERS OF BOLIVIAN DELEGATION. DELEGATION CHIEF MEDEIROS IS ONE OF BOLIVIA’S MOST DISTINGUISHED FORMER DIPLOMATS, HAVING SERVED IN A VARIETY OF DIPLOMATIC ASSIGNEMENTS FROM 1938-1952, AND AGAIN FROM 1964 TO 1969. BORN INTO A PROMINENT SUCRE FAMILY ON OCTOBER 15, 1913, MEDEIROS SUBSEQUENTLY OBTAINED HIS LAW DEGREE IN LOCAL UNIVERSITY AND THEN DID POST-GRADUATE WORK IN ARGENTINA BEFORE EMBARKING ON HIS DIPLOMATIC CAREER. FROM 1938 TO 1952 HE SERVED IN VARYING CAPACITIES WITHIN THE FOREIGN MINISTRY AND IN BUENOS AIRES, SANTIAGO, PARIS, RIO DE JANEIRO, THE UNITED NATIONS, GENEVA AND UNESCO. HIS LAST POST DURING THIS PHASE OF HIS DIPLOMATIC LIFE WAS AS DIRECTOR OF POLITICAL DEPARTMENT OF FOREIGN MINISTRY. MEDEIROS RETIRED INTO PRIVATE LIFE DURING ENTIRE PERIOD OF MNR’S RULE IN BOLIVIA (1952-64), SERVING AS PROFESSOR OF CIVIL LAW AT UNIVERSITY OF SUCRE, PRESIDENT OF NATIONAL BANK OF BOLIVIA, LEGAL ADVISOR TO SUPREME COURT AND VICE RECTOR OF UNIVERSITY OF SUCRE. FOLLOWING FALL OF MNR, HE HEADED BOLIVIAN DELEGATION TO UNITED NATIONS, WAS NAMED AMBASSADOR TO ARGENTINA IN MARCH 1965, AND SUBSEQUENTLY RETURNED TO WORK IN FOREIGN MINISTRY FROM NOVEMBER 1968 UNTIL HIS APPOINTMENT AS FOREIGN MINISTER BY PRESIDENT SILES SALINAS ON MAY 5, 1969. MEDEIROS, A MEMBER OF PRESIDENT’S MODERATELY RIGHT-WING SOCIAL DEMOCRATIC PARTY, HELD THIS POST UNTIL OVERTHROW OF SILES IN SEPTEMBER 1969 WHEN HE AGAIN RETIRED INTO PRIVATE LIFE. MEDEIROS SPEAKS FRENCH AND PORTUGUESE, BUT IS NOT BELIEVED TO SPEAK ENGLISH. FURTHER DETAILS ON HIS CAREER MAY BE FOUND IN EMBASSY FS-405 REPORT OF MAY 16,

  2. IN EMBASSY’S VIEW, MEDEIROS’ CHIEF ROLE AT CARACAS IS LIKELY TO BE THAT OF A DISTINGUISHED FIGUREHEAD, AND HE IS UNLIKELY TO BE PRESENT THROUGH ENTIRE TWO-MONTH CONFERENCE. MEDEIROS’ LONG ABSENCE FROM ACTIVE DIPLOMACY AND HIS PERSONAL LACK OF FAMILIARITY WITH LOS ISSUES WILL FORCE HIM TO RELY HEAVILY ON TREDINNICK, WHO WILL ALMOST CERTAINLY PROVE TO BE LEADING BOLIVIAN NEGOTIATOR ON KEY POINTS AT ISSUE.

  3. FELIPE TREDINNICK WAS BORN ON NOVEMBER 30, 1930 OF A BOLIVIAN MOTHER AND AN ENGLISH FATHER. HE HOLDS LAW DEGREE FROM UNIVERSITY OF BRAZIL AND DOCTORATE IN INTERNATIONAL LAW FROM UNIVERSITY OF MADRID. HE CURRENTLY IS PLANNING TO PUBLISH

HIS 500-PAGE DOCTORAL THESIS: “LAW OF THE SEA AND MARITIME FRONTIERS,” IN SPAIN DURING 1975. TREDINNICK’S PREVIOUS KIPLO- MATIC POSTS SINCE JOINING THE FOREIGN MINISTRY IN 1964 HAVE IN- CLUDED MINISTER-COUNSELOR IN BRASILIA AND MADRID, CHIEF OF DELE- GATION TO 1974 LANDLOCKED NATIONS CONFERENCE IN KAMPALA AND CHIEF OF DELEGATION TO GROUP OF 77 LOS CONFERENCE IN NAIROBI. HE IS ALSO A PROFESSOR OF INTERNATIONAL LAW AT SAN ANDRES UNIVERSITY IN LA PAZ, AND ATTENDS CLASSES AT PRESTIGIOUS SCHOOL OF HIGHER

MILITARY STUDIES. TREDINNICK IS FORMER MEMBER OF FSB AND WAS EXILED TO BRAZIL BY MNR FROM EARLY 1950’S TO 1964 FOR HIS POLITICAL VIEWS; HE IS STILL PRO-FSB AND VIOLENTLY ANTI-MNR IN HIS POLITICS. HE SPEAKS PORTUGUESE AND A SMALL AMOUNT OF ENGLISH.

  1. IN RECENT EXTENSIVE CONTACTS WITH TREDINNICK, EMBASSY OFFICERS HAVE FOUND HIM TO BE SERIOUS, DEDICATED OFFICIAL WITH WIDE KNOWLEDGE OF LOS ISSUES. HE HAS BEEN OPEN AND FRIENDLY IN ALL OF THESE CONTACTS, AND APPEARS TO HAVE HIGHLY FAVORABLE ORIENTATION TOWARD UNITED STATES. TREDINNICK STATES THAT MANY OF HIS FAMILY RELATIVES LIVE IN US, WHERE THEY HOLD ANNUAL REUNION IN WILKES-BARRE, PENNSYLVANIA; HE HIMSELF EXPRESSES GREAT INTEREST IN POSSI- BILITY OF VISITING US AT EARLIEST OPPORTUNITY. TREDINNICK’S ATTITUDES TOWARD BRAZIL ALSO QUITE FAVORABLE, IN VIEW OF FACT THAT HIS WIFE IS BRAZILIAN; HE ALSO FEELS FROM HIS OWN EXPERIENCE IN BRAZIL THAT BRAZILIANS MAKE GOOD DIPLOMATS AND ARE FLEXIBLE ENOUGH TO MAKE NEGOTIATION POSSIBLE OVER POINTS OF DIFFERENCE. FROM HIS RECENT EXPERIENCES AT KAMPALA AND NAIROBI, TREDINNICK CARRIED AWAY STRONGLY NEGATIVE RE- ACTION TO PERUVIAN AND CHILEAN DIPLOMATS, WHOM HE CHARACTERIZES AS LACKING ANY BARGAINING FLEXIBILITY AND FREEDOM TO NEGOTIATE. BREWIN
